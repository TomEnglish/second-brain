## 9. Evidence Hierarchy (Non-Fiction)

| Tier | Description | Language to Use |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 - Strong | Large RCTs, meta-analyses | "Clinical trials demonstrate..." |
| 2 - Moderate | Small RCTs, observational studies | "Human studies suggest..." |
| 3 - Emerging | Animal/in vitro studies | "Animal studies show..." |
| 4 - Theoretical | Mechanistic plausibility | "Based on its mechanism, X may..." |
| 5 - Anecdotal | Case reports | "Some practitioners report..." |

## 11. Formatting Standards

- Use `# ` (H1) for chapter titles
- Use `## ` (H2) for major sections
- Citations: `[1]` with reference list at chapter end
- Poetry/line breaks: Two trailing spaces
- Chapter naming: Consistent (e.g., "Chapter One" or "Chapter 1")
